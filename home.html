Achieve your imagination through your robot

This is the driving vision of the Robotics Club of BRAC University. We are committed to creating a platform where students can explore and develop their passion for robotics, turning imaginative ideas into reality through hands-on experience, collaboration, and mentorship. Our goal is to inspire a new generation of innovators, engineers, and leaders who are prepared to tackle real-world challenges with cutting-edge technology. We strive to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical implementation, empowering students to think critically and innovate fearlessly. Through workshops, competitions, and interdisciplinary projects, we nurture creativity and teamwork while shaping future leaders in the field of robotics.

Our Flagship Projects

BRACU CHONDROBOT I (2011)

Bangladesh's first lunar robot, ChondroBot I, made history by securing 12th place at NASA’s prestigious Lunabotics competition in 2011. Competing on the global stage as the nation’s first-ever lunar excavator, this groundbreaking achievement showcased BRAC University’s innovative prowess. The robot successfully navigated rough simulated lunar terrain, demonstrated advanced control mechanisms, and efficiently collected soil samples. This remarkable success not only highlighted the technical excellence of Bangladeshi students but also inspired future generations to pursue space research and robotics innovation.

BRACU CHONDROBOT II (2012)

Building on its predecessor's remarkable success, ChondroBot II soared to new heights in 2012, finishing as the top Asian team and proudly outperforming global giants such as Harvard and Virginia Tech at NASA’s prestigious Lunabotics competition. With generous sponsorship from ROBI, the project demonstrated not only technical excellence but also the collaborative spirit of BRAC University students and faculty. This outstanding achievement firmly established the university as a pioneer in robotic innovation, inspiring confidence in Bangladesh’s growing space technology potential.

BRAC ONNESHA (2017)

BRAC Onnesha, Bangladesh’s first satellite, was successfully launched from the International Space Station (ISS) in 2017, marking a historic milestone in the nation’s scientific journey. Designed and developed by the talented students of BRAC University, this nanosatellite carried instruments for earth observation, atmospheric research, and communication experiments. By opening the door for Bangladesh’s expanding role in space research and advanced technology, BRAC Onnesha not only showcased national capability but also inspired future innovators to contribute to aerospace exploration.

BRACU OGNIBIR (2023)

Ognibir, Bangladesh's first industrial-grade autonomous robot, stands as a groundbreaking innovation by integrating advanced firefighting and rescue capabilities into a single platform. Designed to operate in hazardous environments, it ensures safety while minimizing human risk. In 2023, Ognibir triumphed as the Champion at AUST Tech Fest, outshining strong competitors and earning national recognition. This remarkable achievement further demonstrated BRAC University’s technical mastery in industrial robotics and highlighted its role in shaping the future of automation and disaster management technologies.

BRACU DUBURI (2018)

BRACU Duburi, Bangladesh's first Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV), marked a groundbreaking milestone in the nation’s robotics history. Despite limited resources and funding challenges, the team secured 2nd place at RoboSub 2023 in the USA, defeating Ivy League institutions and 35+ world-renowned universities. Their passion for technology and commitment to advancing underwater exploration showcased Bangladesh’s rising potential on the global stage. Notable achievements include: 1st Place Website (2024), 2nd Place Overall (2023), Ingenuity Special Award (2023), Best Rookie Team (2022), and 7th Place Overall (2018).

BRACU MONGOL TORI (2017)

BRAC University’s "Mongol-Tori" is Bangladesh’s first Mars rover, developed for the University Rover Challenge (URC) 2018 by a 22-member student team. The project reflects remarkable innovation, teamwork, and engineering resilience, positioning Bangladesh as a rising contributor to space and robotic technology. Over the years, Mongol-Tori has earned international recognition with top achievements, including 2nd Position in Asia at URC 2023, 4th in URC 2021, 3rd in URC 2020, and several other prestigious placements in global competitions and expos.

BRACU NABIK (2024)

Nabik is a remote-controlled robot developed by BRAC University students to assist in critical flood relief operations in Bangladesh. Specifically designed to navigate through submerged and debris-filled areas, it has successfully delivered essential supplies such as food, medicine, and clean water to stranded communities during natural disasters. With the ability to carry payloads of up to 60 kilograms and a battery life of approximately 3 hours, Nabik highlights the potential of robotics in humanitarian aid and disaster management.
